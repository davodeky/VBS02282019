Sunday's Sermon

Bible Reading

Theme: Arise and Shine
Philippians 4:10-13

Sunday's Sermon
  • Paul is so confident that his Lord can never let him down.
  • The bible has so many promises for us and it is a high time we begin believing them.
  • Paul counted himself lucky to be called by God.
  • Paul is letting us know that his trust in God is unshakable.
  • Paul is an example that God is not looking for superhumans to do His work.
  • The lord is telling us that we can do all things through Christ.
  • A "you can do" is a responsibility.
  • This part of the Bible is very often taken out of context.
  • The apostole Paul is not speaking to a moment but to a season.
  • Our problems can't disappear the moment we get saved.
  • However, our problems may not disappear, but God gives us the strength to beat the hardships.
  • God multiplies our strength and will always grant us victory.
  • Like the eagle, it never ask God to stop the rain or a storm, God has given it the ability to rise above the storm.
  • Paul is therefore implying that when problems come, God give him the knowledge, the strength, the strategy to overcome.
  • Prayer increases our strength, it multiplies our capacity
  • God is a sovereign God, He elevates when He wants to, He heals when He wants, He strengths when He wants.
  • We can not arm-twist our God.
  • Our prayers, however, gives us the abilityt to look at our problems and say "I can do all things through Christ Who strengthens me."
  • The world today has minimized the presence of Christ in the giving of strength.
  • There are things that God will not do for us, unless we do them for ourselves.
  • Other people may look prosperous not because God loves them differently, but because they are able to make the effort.
  • Let us be reminded again that "I can do all things through Christ Who strengthens me."
  • We have made prayer a portal for laziness.
